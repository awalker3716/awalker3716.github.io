Who is SZA?

SZA (Sol√°na Imani Robinson) is an American singer, songwriter, and record producer. Born in 1989 in St. Louis, Missouri, she first gained recognition for her music in the early 2010s, releasing her first EP, "See.SZA.Run," in 2012. Since then, she has become one of the leading voices in contemporary R&B and hip-hop, known for her powerful vocals, introspective lyrics, and innovative sound. SZA's debut studio album, "Ctrl," was released in 2017 to critical acclaim, earning her several Grammy nominations and solidifying her place as one of the most exciting and creative musicians of her generation.

Awards and Recognition

  • BET Awards: SZA won the award for Best New Artist in 2018.
  • Grammy Awards: SZA has received five Grammy nominations, including Best Urban Contemporary Album for "Ctrl" and Best Rap/Sung Performance for "All the Stars" with Kendrick Lamar.
  • Billboard Music Awards: SZA received a nomination for Top R&B Female Artist in 2018.
  • MTV Video Music Awards: SZA received a nomination for Best Art Direction for the "Love Galore" music video in 2018.
  • iHeartRadio Music Awards: SZA won the award for Best Lyrics for "The Weekend" in 2018.
  • Soul Train Awards: SZA received a nomination for Best New Artist in 2018.
  • New Music Seminar: SZA won the award for Artist on the Verge in 2013.

This list is not exhaustive and only represents a selection of the awards that SZA has won. It's important to note that SZA's musical achievements and impact extend far beyond awards, and her contributions to the music industry and popular culture are significant.
